8 WARNING SIGNS YOU NEED A PLUMBER


No one likes to think about the possibility of a plumbing emergency. But the truth is, plumbing problems can happen anytime—and when they do, they can be a real headache. The good news is that most plumbing problems are relatively easy to fix if you catch them early. Here are eight warning signs that you need to call a plumber:

1. Water-stained Ceilings or Walls


If you notice water stains on your ceiling or walls, it indicates a leak in your pipes. While small leaks might not seem like a big deal, they can quickly become more significant and expensive problems if they’re not fixed immediately. If you have a water leak, don’t wait to get it fixed. The sooner you call a plumber, the sooner your problem will be resolved.

Water leaks can cause all sorts of problems in your home. Not only can they damage your walls and ceilings, but they can also lead to mold growth. Mold is a severe health hazard and can be tough to get rid of once it starts growing. If you have a water leak, you must call a plumber immediately so the problem can be fixed before it leads to mold growth.

2. Low Water Pressure


If you turn on your faucet and the water pressure is lower than usual, it’s likely due to a sediment build-up in your pipes. Over time, minerals and other particles can accumulate in your pipes, causing the water pressure to drop. In most cases, you can clear the sediment by flushing your pipes.

  1. Turn on all the faucets in your home to allow the water to start flowing through the pipes.
  2. Locate the main shut-off valve for your home and turn it on.
  3. Turn on the outside hose bib and let the water run for a few minutes.
  4. Check all the faucets in your home to see if the water pressure has increased. If not, repeat steps 1-3.

3. Leaking Faucets or Pipes


A dripping faucet might not seem like a big deal, but it wastes a lot of water (and money) over time. Plus, if left unaddressed, leaking faucets and pipes can cause significant damage to your home.

Here are a few tips to help you fix a dripping faucet:

  1. Identify the source of the leak. Is it coming from the handle, the spout, or somewhere else?
  2. Once you know where the leak is coming from, you can try to tighten the connections. This is often the most straightforward and quickest fix.
  3. If tightening the connections doesn’t work, you may need to replace worn-out washers or gaskets.
  4. Sometimes, you may need to replace the entire faucet. This is usually only necessary if the tap is old or damaged beyond repair.

4. Slow Draining Sinks or showers


If your sink or shower drains more slowly than usual, it’s likely due to a clog in your pipes. Clogs can be caused by anything from hair buildup to food scraps, and they’re usually pretty easy to fix.

There are a few different ways that you can try to clear a clog, depending on where it’s located and how severe it is. For a minor clog, you might be able to remove it yourself with a plunger or a plumber’s snake. If the clog is more severe, you may need to call a professional plumber to take care of it.

Strange Noises Coming from Your Pipes


If you hear strange noises from your pipes (like banging, ticking, or whistling), it might be due to high water pressure or an obstruction in the pipe. In either case, it’s best to have a professional look at the problem to fix it before it leads to more extensive (and expensive) damage.

If you have a water pressure regulator, check to see if it’s working properly. If the regulator is damaged or not functioning correctly, it could cause your pipes to make strange noises. You can usually find the water pressure regulator near your home’s main water line.

6. Running Toilets


A running toilet is not only annoying—but it’s also wasting precious water (and money). If your toilet won’t stop running after you flush it, there’s likely something wrong with the fill or flapper valve. These are relatively easy problems for our plumbers to fix—but if left unaddressed, they’ll only get worse over time (and end up costing you more money)

7. Unexplained spikes in Your Water Bill

If you notice that your water bill has increased significantly for no apparent reason (.e., you haven’t been watering your lawn more), it could be due to a hidden leak in your plumbing system. Leaks can be tricky to find because they’re often hidden behind walls or under floors—but our plumbers have the experience and expertise necessary to find them and fix them quickly (before they cause too much damage).

If you’re concerned about a leak on your property, the best thing to do is call a professional plumber for an inspection. Our team at ABC Plumbing will be happy to come out and take a look—we have the tools and expertise necessary to locate and fix any leaks quickly.

8. Mold Growth

Mold is not only unsightly—it can also pose serious health risks. Notice mold growing anywhere in your home (particularly in areas with plumbing). It could be due to excess moisture in the air (caused by leaks) or condensation (caused by insufficient ventilation).
